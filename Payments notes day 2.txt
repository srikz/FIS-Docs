Authorization actual working backend:
	cardholder payes merchant
	merchant sends card details to front end processor
	the FEP trnsfers card details to appropriate card network
	card network identifies the issusing bank and requests payment authorization
	if funds are available on the card issuing bank sends approval code to merchant 
	the merchants payment terminal collects all authorization in a batch
	if approved the merchant completes the sale and the customer receives a receipt

capture:
	the merchant sends the card details ina batch to the acquirers front end processor
	the front end processor frowards the batch to the backend processor

clearing:
	after completing copience and security checks backend processor forwards the batch to the card network
	the card network send each approved transaction to the appropriate issuing bank

settlement:
	issuer bank releases funds through the card network
	the card network pay the processor its percentage of remaning funds
	the processor credits the acquirer the original amount
	the acquirer credits the merchant

Risks in trade:
	Counterparty Risk -> buyer faces the risk that sller might not supply on time 
				or migght supply spurious goods. seller faces the risk that buyer might not pay on time

	Country risk -> even when buyer is willing t pay they might be unable to pay due to restrictions on foreign exchange 
				transaction imposed by his country

	legal risk -> contractual terms might be interpreted differently in defferrent countries parties may not get the level 
				of protection that they anticipate

	exchange risk -> fluctuations in exchange rates might cause profits to go down or costs to go up

Letter of credit
	 A payment undertaking provided by a buyers bank to a seller, on behalf of the buyer, to effect paymnet to 
	the seller provided the seller complies with the documentary requirements required under the credit

parties to a letter of credit
	applicant/buyer
	beneficiary

LC issuance process
	sales contract between applicant and beneficiary
	applicant submitting LC application to issuing bank
	Issusing bank LC issuance charge to advising bank
	advising bank advising the beneficiary

Confirmation of LC
	process whereby a bank other than the issuing bank adds their payment confirmation to an already issued Lc
	
	done in situattions where the beneficiary might not be comfortable with the credit rating of the existing 
	issuing bank
	
	the confirmation added to the Lc assures the supplier that in case of failure of issuing bank to honour the 
	payment commitment the confirming bank will step in
